Setting · Cadre

A small basin, two big users

The Lez basin covers around 711 km². The Lez source supplies roughly 25 million m³ of drinking water to Montpellier each year. Surrounding farmland — mostly vineyards, with cereals and orchards mixed in — adds its own demand on the same karstic aquifer. Cropland is roughly 14% of the basin; meadow, urban, forest and water make up most of the rest.

Finding · Constat

Crops use water differently

Per square metre of cropland over the 2018 growing season (NW-quarter pyWaPOR run, full-basin OSO areas):

  • Vineyards (largest cropland share): moderate AETI per area
  • Orchards (olives + stone fruit, ~60/40 mix assumed): higher AETI
  • Cereals (winter wheat / barley): mid-range AETI
  • Grasslands: high AETI per area

Tension · Tension

Summer is when everyone competes

Crop water demand peaks in July and August — exactly when Montpellier draws most heavily on the Lez source, and when the karstic aquifer is at its lowest. The stress is not annual; it is concentrated.
